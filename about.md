## Hi, I'm Amara.

I'm a writer based in Reading, England. I started this site because I wanted a place that was entirely mine — not a social platform, not a newsletter aggregator, just a quiet corner where I could put words and watch them stay.

I write about the things that catch my attention: a stranger's phrase on the bus, the particular light of a Tuesday afternoon, a book that rewired how I think about everything. Big and small. Heavy and light.

Every week I publish a new piece in the [Chronicles](/chronicles/) section. Some are essays, some are observations, some are something harder to categorise. I try to make each one worth the time it takes to read.

### Why "Chronicles"?

Because a blog felt too technical and a diary too private. A chronicle implies faithfulness to what actually happened — no spin, no performance, just the honest account. That's the aspiration, anyway.

### The writing process

I write a rough draft on Friday, let it sit, revise on Sunday, and publish Monday mornings. The deadline is self-imposed but I take it seriously. Constraints produce work; infinite time produces nothing.
